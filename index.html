I am an AI Research Scientist at Meta, based in Amsterdam. My current research focuses on code generation and reinforcement learning.

I received a BSc in Computer Science from Utrecht University, and an MSc in AI and PhD in Machine Learning (with Prof. Max Welling) from the University of Amsterdam — all three cum laude.

During my PhD, I developed and popularized equivariant networks and geometric deep learning, including Group Equivariant CNNs (G-CNNs), Steerable CNNs, Spherical CNNs, and Gauge Equivariant CNNs on manifolds. These methods leverage symmetries of a learning problem to improve data efficiency and have found applications in medical imaging, drug design, materials science, protein structure analysis, climate modeling, and more.

I co-authored the Geometric Deep Learning book (with M. Bronstein, J. Bruna, P. Veličković), and co-wrote the accompanying lecture course.

I was a co-founder of Scyfer, a deep active learning company acquired by Qualcomm in 2017. At Qualcomm, I founded and led the generative models & data compression team. During my studies I interned at Google DeepMind (with Geoff Hinton) and OpenAI.
